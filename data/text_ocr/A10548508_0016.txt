BUREAU OF IMMIGRATION WARRANT-ARREST OF ALIEN Form 8 C-1 United States of America 7000/348-A DEPARTMENT OF LABOR No. 55697/212 WASHINGTON To DISTRICT DIRECTOR OF IMMIGRATION, El Paso, Texas, Or to any Immigrant Inspector in the service of the United States. WHEREAS, from evidence submitted to me, it appears that the alien IRENEO MORENO, near who landed at the port of Fabens, Texas, on the 3rd day of April, 1930 , is subject to be taken into custody and returned to the country whence he came under section 19 of the immigration act of February 5, 1917, being subject to deportation under the provisions of a law of the United States, to wit: The immigration act approved May 26, 1924, in that he was not at the time of entry into the United States in possession of an unexpired immigration visa, APR 28 1930 and, WHEREAS, from the evidence submitted to me, it appears that the said alien has been found in the United States in violation of the immigration act of February 5, 1917, for the following among other reasons: That he was a person likely to become a public charge at the time of his entry; and that he entered the United States without inspection by land at a place other than a designated port of entry for aliens, I, W. N. SMELSER, Assistant to the Secretary of Labor, by virtue of the power and authority vested in me by the laws of the United States, do hereby command you to take into custody the said alien and grant him a hearing to enable him to show cause why he should not be deported in conformity with law. The expenses of detention hereunder, if necessary, are authorized, payable from the appropriation "Expenses of Regulating Immigration, 1930 " Pending further proceedings the alien may be released from custody under bond in the sum of $500. For so doing, this shall be your sufficient warrant. Witness my hand and seal this 18thday of April, 1930. AEC 14-2204 U.S. GOVERNMENT PRINTING OFFICE 1929 Assistant to the Secretary of Labor.
