DEPARTMENT OF LABOR IMMIGRATION SERVICE SUMMARY: It is found from the evidence that Ireneo Moreno, or Ireno Moreno, the person made the subject of these proceedings, is an alien, a native and citizen of Mexico, who last entered the United States from Mexico near the port of Fabens, Texas on April 3, 1930, in violation of law, and may be deported for the follow- ing reasons, to-wit: (1) That he entered by land at a place other than a designated port of entry for aliens. (2) That he was a person likely to become a public charge at time of entry. (3) That he is in the United States in violation of the Immigration Act of 1924, in that he was not in possession of an unexpired immigration visa at time of entry. RECOMMENDATION: It is respectfully recommended that the alien be deported to Mexico, the country whence he came and of which he is a native and citizen. LOCATION: El Paso County Jail, El Paso, Texas, at the expense of the Department of Justice, awaiting trial for violation of Section 2 of the Immigration Act of March 4,1929, warrant, therefore, if and when issued, should provide for stay of execution.
