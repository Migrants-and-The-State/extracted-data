Form I-215W (3-27-67) UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE IMMIGRATION AND NATURALIZATION SERVICE RECORD OF SWORN STATEMENT IN AFFIDAVIT FORM AFFIDAVIT--WITNESS IN RE: Leopoldo BEEKS FILE NO. A17 225 500 EXECUTED AT Los Angeles, Calif. INV DATE April 27, 1970 Before the following officer of the U.S. Immigration and Naturalization Service: in the English language. Interpreter used. I, Leopold Beeks , acknowledge that the above-named officer has identified himself to me as an officer of the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service authorized by law to administer oaths and take testimony in connection with the enforcement of the Immigration and Nationality laws of the United States. He has informed me that he desires to take my sworn statement regarding my Xxxxy status here in the US under the Immigration Laws of the US He has told me that my statement must be made freely and voluntarily. I am willing to make such a statement. I swear that I will tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, so helpme, God. Being duly sworn, I make the following statement: I admit that I am a citizen of and that I am illegally in the United States. I have been informed that I have been granted voluntary departure pursuant to my request that the Immigration and Naturalization Service allow me to return to I agree that my custody and my departure be under safeguards, with the understanding that my return to will be accomplished by the earliest available transportation, at no expense to myself. I understand that if my application to depart the United States at government expense is granted, and if I am removed at no expense to myself, I will be ineligible to apply for or to receive a visa or other documents for readmission, or to apply for admission to the United States, except with prior approval of the Attorney General. Voluntary departure at Government expense - own expense - authorized. Allan G. Juhl Assistant District Director for Investigations April 27, 1970 Date
